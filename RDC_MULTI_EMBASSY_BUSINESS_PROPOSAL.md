# RDC Multi-Embassy Platform Business Proposal

## Executive Summary

This proposal presents a clear path to evolve the current E-Ambassade application into a shared digital platform for RDC embassies abroad.

The current solution already demonstrates strong practical value as a pilot for one embassy. With targeted upgrades, it can become a reusable national platform supporting multiple RDC embassies in Europe and beyond.

The recommended strategy is to transform the existing application into one configurable multi-embassy platform rather than creating separate disconnected systems for each embassy.

## Strategic Opportunity

RDC embassies abroad often face similar operational challenges:

- appointment scheduling by phone or e-mail
- inconsistent communication with applicants
- limited visibility into appointment volume
- difficulty standardizing service information
- time lost in repetitive manual coordination

A shared digital platform addresses these challenges by giving embassies a common operational tool while preserving each embassy's local identity and local service configuration.

## Vision

The target vision is a centralized RDC consular platform that can serve multiple embassies abroad from one shared system.

Each embassy would have:

- its own branded public presence
- its own services and appointment rules
- its own staff and administrators
- its own reports and operational visibility
- multilingual communication adapted to its host country

Citizens would benefit from a more consistent and modern consular experience across different countries.

## Why This Matters

This is more than a website project. It is an operational modernization initiative.

The business value includes:

- improved public service delivery
- reduced administrative friction
- better scheduling discipline
- more consistent communication with applicants
- stronger reporting and accountability
- a scalable model for future embassy onboarding

## Current Project Value

The current application already provides a meaningful foundation because it includes:

- user registration and sign-in
- online booking workflow
- user dashboard
- admin dashboard
- notifications
- multilingual support

This means the project is not starting from zero. It already proves the core workflow and can be positioned as a pilot with a credible expansion path.

## Recommended Strategic Direction

The recommended direction is:

Build one shared RDC consular platform with embassy-specific configuration.

This is preferable to developing separate tools for each embassy because it:

- reduces long-term cost
- improves consistency
- centralizes maintenance and upgrades
- simplifies training and support
- allows gradual onboarding of new embassies

## Scope of the Expansion

To support RDC embassies abroad, the platform should be expanded in the following areas.

### 1. Embassy Management

The system must support multiple embassies as separate operational entities.

Each embassy should have:

- official identity and branding
- address and contact information
- office hours and local calendar
- supported languages
- configured services

### 2. Consular Services Structure

The service model should reflect real consular activity such as:

- visa requests
- passport renewal
- consular card requests
- birth and marriage registration
- document legalization
- emergency travel documents
- power of attorney and civil documentation

Each embassy should be able to enable only the services it actually offers.

### 3. Role-Based Operations

The platform should support different categories of users:

- platform administrators
- embassy administrators
- embassy staff
- citizen applicants

This creates a clearer governance model and better operational control.

### 4. Communications and Public Information

Each embassy should be able to publish and manage:

- service instructions
- required documents
- office announcements
- contact details
- appointment reminders and confirmations

This improves clarity for citizens and reduces repeated manual questions.

### 5. Reporting and Oversight

The platform should provide management visibility such as:

- number of bookings by embassy
- service demand by type
- appointment status tracking
- historical activity reports
- administrative audit trails

## Expected Benefits

### Benefits for Embassies

- less manual workload
- better organization of appointments
- improved communication with citizens
- stronger visibility into demand and operations
- reusable digital infrastructure for future growth

### Benefits for Citizens

- easier access to consular appointments
- clearer information before visiting the embassy
- multilingual interaction
- better follow-up and notification flow
- more predictable service experience

### Benefits for the RDC Network Abroad

- stronger institutional consistency
- lower cost than building separate platforms repeatedly
- easier training and support across missions
- shared data standards and reporting logic

## Recommended Delivery Approach

### Phase 1: Foundation Upgrade

Upgrade the current single-embassy application so that it can support multiple embassies with separate data and permissions.

### Phase 2: Embassy-Specific Operations

Add configurable services, schedules, communications, and public information pages for each embassy.

### Phase 3: Production Hardening

Reinforce security, auditability, monitoring, and operational readiness for official use.

### Phase 4: Progressive Rollout

Onboard embassies gradually, starting with Stockholm as the reference deployment and then extending to additional embassies such as Brussels or Paris.

## Recommended Rollout Model

Suggested rollout sequence:

1. Stockholm as the first reference embassy
2. One second embassy with different operational needs
3. Validation of the shared platform model
4. Structured onboarding of further embassies abroad

This reduces risk and makes it possible to improve the platform before wider expansion.

## Governance Recommendation

The platform should be managed with a clear governance structure covering:

- platform ownership
- embassy onboarding process
- support responsibilities
- role assignment rules
- data protection and access review
- release and change management

This is important because a multi-embassy platform is both a technical system and an operational program.

## Investment Perspective

The investment should be seen in three layers:

- digital modernization of one embassy
- creation of a reusable platform for multiple embassies
- long-term reduction of duplication across missions abroad

A shared platform has higher strategic value than a single local implementation because each future onboarding becomes cheaper than the first.

## Risk Awareness

The expansion should be managed carefully. Main risks include:

- weak tenant separation between embassies
- inconsistent service rules across countries
- insufficient security hardening for personal data
- underestimating change management and staff adoption

These risks are manageable if the rollout is phased and the architecture is designed correctly from the start.

## Final Recommendation

The current E-Ambassade project should be positioned as a practical pilot and a strong foundation for a broader RDC embassy digital platform.

The recommended next step is not to reproduce the Stockholm solution embassy by embassy. The recommended next step is to invest in a controlled architecture upgrade that makes the platform reusable across RDC embassies abroad.

This approach delivers:

- stronger institutional value
- lower long-term cost
- better operational standardization
- a more scalable digital service model for citizens abroad

## Suggested Immediate Decision

Approve a multi-embassy transformation package covering:

- technical architecture upgrade
- data model redesign
- embassy-scoped permissions
- configurable service and calendar management
- security and audit hardening
- pilot rollout for one additional embassy after Stockholm
